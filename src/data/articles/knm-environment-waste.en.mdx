      </p>
      <p>
        Zet je het vuilnis op de verkeerde dag buiten of scheid je het niet goed? Dan kun je een <strong>boete</strong> krijgen van de gemeente.

      <h2>Separating waste</h2>
      <p>
        In the Netherlands, you must <strong>separate waste (afval scheiden)</strong>. This is better for the environment because many materials can be reused.
      </p>
      <p>
        There are different types of waste:
      </p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>GFT</strong> (Vegetable, Fruit, and Garden waste): this goes in the green bin.</li>
        <li><strong>Paper (Papier)</strong> and cardboard: this goes in the paper bin.</li>
        <li><strong>Glass (Glas)</strong>: you take this to the glass recycling container.</li>
        <li><strong>Chemical waste (Chemisch afval)</strong> (such as batteries, paint, and medicines): hand this in separately at a collection point or recycling center.</li>
        <li><strong>Residual waste (Restafval)</strong>: everything that remains goes in the grey bin.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Rules and fines</h2>
      <p>
        Every municipality has its own rules. You can check the <strong>waste calendar (afvalkalender)</strong> to see on which day the garbage is collected.
      </p>
      <p>
        If you put the garbage out on the wrong day or do not separate it correctly, you can get a <strong>fine (boete)</strong> from the municipality.
      </p>
