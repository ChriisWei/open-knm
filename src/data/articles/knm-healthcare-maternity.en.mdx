      <h2>Pregnancy and birth</h2>
      <p>
        When you are pregnant, you usually do not go to the family doctor, but to the <strong>midwife (verloskundige)</strong>. She regularly checks the health of you and the baby.
      </p>
      <p>
        In the Netherlands, you can give birth at home or in the hospital. After the birth, you receive help at home for a week. This is called <strong>maternity care (kraamzorg)</strong>. The maternity nurse teaches you how to care for the baby and checks your health.
      </p>

      <h2>The child health center</h2>
      <p>
        Parents with young children (0 to 4 years old) regularly go to the <strong>child health center (consultatiebureau)</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        A doctor or nurse checks whether the child is growing well and developing properly. The child also receives free <strong>vaccinations (vaccinaties)</strong> here against serious diseases.
      </p>
