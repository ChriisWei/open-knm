    <div className="space-y-4">
      <h2>Social etiquette and visiting</h2>
      <p>
        When you meet someone for the first time, you shake <strong>hands (hand geven)</strong>. Family and friends often give each other <strong>three kisses (drie kussen)</strong> on the cheek.
      </p>
      <p>
        Dutch people find it very important to be <strong>on time (op tijd)</strong>. Are you late? That is considered impolite. Call to let them know.
      </p>
      <p>
        Are you visiting someone? Make an <strong>appointment (afspraak)</strong> first. You usually do not just drop by.
      </p>

      <h2>Problems and help</h2>
      <p>
        Do you have trouble with your neighbors (for example, loud music)? Go <strong>talk (praten)</strong> to the neighbors first. Try to solve it together.
      </p>
      <p>
        Are you being discriminated against? You can ask for help at the <strong>Anti-Discrimination Bureau (ADB)</strong>.
      </p>
      <p>
        For free legal advice, you can go to the <strong>Legal Counter (Juridisch Loket)</strong>. If you are a victim of a crime, you must <strong>report it (aangifte doen)</strong> to the police.
      </p>
